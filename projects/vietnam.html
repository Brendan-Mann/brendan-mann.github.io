GIS 309 — Thematic Mapping

Bombing Missions of
the Vietnam War

Western Illinois University  ·  February 2026

Between 1965 and 1975, the United States and its allies dropped millions of bombs on Vietnam, Laos, and Cambodia — one of the largest aerial bombardments in history. This map, built from declassified THOR mission reports, visualizes every recorded bombing sortie with available geographic data, overlaid on a hillshade relief base and paired with a temporal bar chart of monthly mission totals.

ArcGIS Pro THOR Database SRTM Hillshade Dot Density Historical Cartography

Map

Data Source

Theater History of Operations (THOR) database, declassified U.S. Air Force mission reports digitized by the Defense Digital Service. Each record represents a single bombing sortie with coordinates, date, aircraft type, and ordnance. Records with missing or incomplete geographic data were excluded.

Dot Density

Each dot represents one recorded bombing sortie. No aggregation was applied — the raw point density directly conveys the spatial concentration of missions. The Ho Chi Minh trail corridor through Laos and the coastal lowlands of South Vietnam are immediately legible as the highest-intensity zones.

Base Map

SRTM 30m hillshade relief provides topographic context, making the relationship between bombing patterns and terrain — particularly the trail corridor through the Annamite Mountains — visually apparent. Surrounding countries are rendered in muted gray to focus attention on the three bombed nations.

Design

Purple dot density on a gray relief base, with orange boundary highlighting for Vietnam, Laos, and Cambodia. The purple-orange palette avoids the red-war-map cliché while maintaining visual weight. An embedded bar chart shows monthly mission totals 1965–1975, providing temporal context for the spatial pattern — the 1968–1969 peak corresponds to the Tet Offensive period and subsequent escalation.

Built with QGIS, plain HTML, and strong opinions about color palettes.
